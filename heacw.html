üî¨ Structure of Human Eye

Key Parts of the Eye:

  • Cornea: Transparent front layer that bends light
  • Iris: Colored part that controls light entry
  • Pupil: Opening that allows light to enter
  • Lens: Focuses light onto the retina
  • Retina: Light-sensitive screen where images form
  • Optic Nerve: Carries signals to the brain

üö´ Defects of Vision

üòµ Myopia (Short-sightedness)

Problem: Can see near objects clearly but not distant objects

Cause: Eyeball too long or lens too curved

Correction: Concave lens

Power = -1/f (negative)

üò§ Hypermetropia (Long-sightedness)

Problem: Can see distant objects clearly but not near objects

Cause: Eyeball too short or lens too flat

Correction: Convex lens

Power = +1/f (positive)

üë¥ Presbyopia (Old-age vision)

Problem: Both near and far vision affected

Cause: Loss of accommodation power with age

Correction: Bifocal lenses

üåÖ Atmospheric Refraction

üåÖ Advanced Sunrise & Delayed Sunset

Due to atmospheric refraction, we see the sun 2 minutes before actual sunrise and 2 minutes after actual sunset.

‚≠ê Twinkling of Stars

Stars twinkle due to continuous refraction of light through changing air layers in the atmosphere.

üåô Why Planets Don't Twinkle

Planets appear as disks (not point sources) so the twinkling effect gets averaged out.

üåà Scattering of Light

üîµ Why Sky is Blue?

During sunrise/sunset, light travels longer path. Blue light gets scattered away, only red light reaches us.
