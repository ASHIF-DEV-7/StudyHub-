Human Eye and the Colourful World

Structure of the Human Eye

  • Definition: Light-sensitive organ enabling vision, works like a camera.
  • Parts: Cornea (refracts light), Iris (controls pupil size), Lens (focuses light), Retina (image formation), Optic nerve (transmits signals).
  • Function: Light enters cornea, focused by lens, forms image on retina, signals sent to brain.
  • Examples:
    • Cornea bends light to focus on retina (like a camera lens).
    • Iris adjusts pupil in bright light (e.g., sunny day) vs. dim light (e.g., night).
    • Retina detects colors in a rainbow due to cone cells.

Power of Accommodation

  • Definition: Ability of the eye lens to adjust focal length for near and far objects.
  • Mechanism: Ciliary muscles change lens shape (thicker for near, thinner for far).
  • Range: Near point (25 cm), far point (infinity).
  • Examples:
    • Reading a book (lens thickens for 25 cm focus).
    • Viewing distant stars (lens thins for infinity).
    • Driving (lens adjusts for road signs at varying distances).

Defects of Vision

  • Myopia (Near-sightedness): Sees near objects clearly, far objects blurry; corrected with concave lens.
  • Hypermetropia (Far-sightedness): Sees far objects clearly, near objects blurry; corrected with convex lens.
  • Presbyopia: Age-related loss of accommodation; corrected with bifocal lens.
  • Examples:
    • Myopia: Difficulty reading road signs (corrected with concave glasses).
    • Hypermetropia: Struggling to read a book (corrected with convex glasses).
    • Presbyopia: Elderly needing bifocals for reading and distance.

Refraction of Light through a Prism

  • Definition: Light bends when passing through a prism, splitting into colors (dispersion).
  • Angle of deviation: Depends on angle of incidence and prism material.
  • Spectrum: Violet bends most, red least (VIBGYOR).
  • Examples:
    • Rainbow formation (water droplets act as prisms).
    • Prism in a spectrometer splits light into colors.
    • Decorative glass prisms create rainbow patterns.

Dispersion of Light

  • Definition: Splitting of white light into its constituent colors (VIBGYOR).
  • Cause: Different wavelengths refract differently (violet shortest, red longest).
  • Applications: Spectroscopy, rainbow formation, optical instruments.
  • Examples:
    • Rainbow after rain due to dispersion in water droplets.
    • Prism creating a spectrum on a wall.
    • CD reflecting light in rainbow colors.

Scattering of Light

  • Definition: Light spreads in all directions when passing through particles (e.g., air molecules).
  • Tyndall Effect: Scattering by colloidal particles, visible beam in fog.
  • Applications: Blue sky, red sunset, fog lights.
  • Examples:
    • Blue sky due to scattering of shorter blue wavelengths.
    • Red sunset as longer red wavelengths pass through atmosphere.
    • Headlight beams visible in fog (Tyndall effect).

Atmospheric Refraction

  • Definition: Bending of light due to varying refractive index of atmosphere.
  • Effects: Twinkling of stars, early sunrise, delayed sunset.
  • Cause: Density of air decreases with altitude, bending light.
  • Examples:
    • Stars twinkle due to atmospheric turbulence.
    • Sun visible before actual sunrise due to refraction.
    • Mirage in deserts (hot air refracts light).
