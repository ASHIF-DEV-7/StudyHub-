Metals and Non-Metals

Physical Properties of Metals
  • Shiny (lustrous), good conductors of heat and electricity, malleable, ductile, solid (except Hg).
  • High melting/boiling points, high density (except alkali metals).
  • Examples:
    • Copper: Shiny, conducts electricity (used in wires).
    • Iron: Malleable, used in construction.
    • Aluminium: Ductile, drawn into wires.
Physical Properties of Non-Metals
  • Dull, poor conductors, brittle, low melting/boiling points, low density (except diamond).
  • Can be solid, liquid, or gas.
  • Examples:
    • Carbon (graphite): Brittle, conducts electricity slightly.
    • Sulphur: Dull, poor conductor, brittle solid.
    • Bromine: Liquid, non-conductor.
Chemical Properties of Metals
  • Form basic oxides, lose electrons (form cations), react with acids to form H₂.
  • Examples:
    • Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂ (reaction with acid).
    • 2Na + O₂ → Na₂O (basic oxide formation).
    • Mg + H₂SO₄ → MgSO₄ + H₂ (hydrogen gas production).
Chemical Properties of Non-Metals
  • Form acidic or neutral oxides, gain electrons (form anions), do not react with acids.
  • Examples:
    • C + O₂ → CO₂ (acidic oxide).
    • 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O (neutral oxide).
    • S + O₂ → SO₂ (acidic oxide).
Reactivity Series
  • Order of metals by reactivity: K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Pb > H > Cu > Hg > Ag > Au.
  • More reactive metals displace less reactive ones from compounds.
  • Examples:
    • Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu (Zn more reactive than Cu).
    • Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu (Fe displaces Cu).
    • Cu + AgNO₃ → Cu(NO₃)₂ + Ag (Cu displaces Ag).
Extraction of Metals
  • Highly reactive: Electrolysis (e.g., Na, K).
  • Moderately reactive: Reduction with carbon (e.g., Zn, Fe).
  • Low reactive: Roasting/smelting (e.g., Cu, Hg).
  • Examples:
    • NaCl → Na + Cl₂ (electrolysis for sodium).
    • Fe₂O₃ + 3C → 2Fe + 3CO (reduction for iron).
    • HgS → Hg + S (roasting for mercury).
Refining of Metals
  • Purification of extracted metals (e.g., electrolytic refining).
  • Process: Impure metal (anode), pure metal (cathode), electrolyte.
  • Examples:
    • Copper refining: CuSO₄ electrolyte, pure Cu deposited.
    • Aluminium refining: Electrolysis in molten Al₂O₃.
    • Zinc refining: ZnSO₄ electrolyte used.
Corrosion
  • Deterioration of metals by chemical reaction with environment.
  • Prevention: Painting, galvanizing, alloying.
  • Examples:
    • Rusting: 4Fe + 3O₂ + 2xH₂O → 2Fe₂O₃·xH₂O.
    • Copper patina: 2Cu + O₂ + CO₂ + H₂O → Cu₂(OH)₂CO₃.
    • Silver tarnishing: 2Ag + H₂S → Ag₂S + H₂.
Alloys
  • Mixture of metals or metal with non-metal, enhances properties.
  • Examples:
    • Steel: Fe + C (strong, used in construction).
    • Brass: Cu + Zn (corrosion-resistant, decorative).
    • Bronze: Cu + Sn (durable, used in statues).
Uses of Metals and Non-Metals
  • Metals:
    • Copper: Electrical wires, utensils.
    • Iron: Construction, machinery.
    • Aluminium: Aircraft, packaging.
  • Non-Metals:
    • Carbon: Fuel (coal), electrodes (graphite).
    • Oxygen: Respiration, combustion.
    • Nitrogen: Fertilizers, explosives.
