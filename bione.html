Equation: 6CO‚ÇÇ + 6H‚ÇÇO ‚Üí C‚ÇÜH‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇO‚ÇÜ + 6O‚ÇÇ (in presence of light & chlorophyll)

Nutrition in Humans

  1. Mouth: Teeth and salivary glands ‚Äì chewing, amylase breaks down starch.
  2. Pharynx & Oesophagus: Movement of food (peristalsis).
  3. Stomach: HCl and pepsin ‚Äì partial digestion of proteins.
  4. Small Intestine: Bile (liver), pancreatic juice, and intestinal juice digest carbohydrates, proteins, and fats completely.
  5. Villi: Absorption of digested food.
  6. Large Intestine: Reabsorption of water.

Human Respiratory System

  1. Nasal Cavity: Filters, moistens, and warms air.
  2. Pharynx: Passage of air.
  3. Larynx: Voice box.

  • Inhalation: Diaphragm contracts and moves down, ribs move up ‚Üí chest cavity expands ‚Üí air enters lungs.

ü©∏ Transportation

Definition: The process of transporting essential substances such as food, water, gases, and wastes within the body.

Transportation in Humans

  • Blood: Fluid connective tissue, consists of plasma, RBCs, WBCs, and platelets.
  • Functions:
    • Transports O‚ÇÇ from lungs to body and CO‚ÇÇ back to lungs.
    • Transports nutrients from digestive system to body cells.
    • Transports hormones and enzymes.
    • Removes waste products.
  • Heart: Muscular organ that pumps blood.
    • Four chambers ‚Äì Right atrium, Right ventricle, Left atrium, Left ventricle.
    • Valves ensure one-way flow of blood.
    • Double circulation: Pulmonary (heart ‚Üí lungs ‚Üí heart) + Systemic (heart ‚Üí body ‚Üí heart).
  • Blood vessels:
    • Arteries ‚Äì Carry blood away from heart (oxygenated, except pulmonary artery).
    • Veins ‚Äì Carry blood to heart (deoxygenated, except pulmonary vein).
    • Capillaries ‚Äì Thin vessels for exchange of materials.

Transportation in Plants

  • Xylem: Transports water and minerals from roots to leaves.
  • Phloem: Transports food (sugar) from leaves to other parts of the plant.
  • Transpiration: Loss of water as vapor through stomata ‚Üí helps in upward movement of water.
