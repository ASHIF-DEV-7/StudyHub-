Class 10 Magnetism Formulas

1. Magnetic Force on a Current-Carrying Conductor

\[ F = I \cdot L \cdot B \cdot \sin\theta \]

Where:

F: Force (N), I: Current (A), L: Length (m), B: Magnetic field (T), θ: Angle between conductor and field

2. Magnetic Field Due to a Straight Conductor

\[ B = \frac{\mu_0 \cdot I}{2 \pi r} \]

Where:

B: Magnetic field (T), μ₀: Permeability of free space (4π × 10⁻⁷ T·m/A), I: Current (A), r: Distance (m)

3. Magnetic Field at the Center of a Circular Loop

\[ B = \frac{\mu_0 \cdot I}{2 r} \]

Where:

B: Magnetic field (T), μ₀: Permeability of free space, I: Current (A), r: Radius (m)

4. Force Between Two Parallel Conductors

\[ F = \frac{\mu_0 \cdot I_1 \cdot I_2 \cdot L}{2 \pi d} \]

Where:

F: Force per unit length (N/m), I₁, I₂: Currents (A), L: Length (m), d: Distance (m)

5. Faraday’s Law of Electromagnetic Induction

\[ \mathcal{E} = -N \cdot \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \]

Where:

ε: Induced EMF (V), N: Number of turns, Φ_B: Magnetic flux (Wb), t: Time (s)

6. Magnetic Flux

\[ \Phi_B = B \cdot A \cdot \cos\theta \]

Where:

Φ_B: Magnetic flux (Wb), B: Magnetic field (T), A: Area (m²), θ: Angle between field and normal
