Electricity

Electric Current
  • Flow of electric charge (electrons) through a conductor.
  • Unit: Ampere (A), measured by ammeter.
  • Formula: I = Q/t (Current = Charge/Time).
  • Examples:
    • 2 C charge flows in 1 s: I = 2/1 = 2 A.
    • 10 C in 5 s: I = 10/5 = 2 A.
    • 6 C in 3 s: I = 6/3 = 2 A.
Electric Potential and Potential Difference
  • Electric potential: Work done per unit charge (V = W/Q).
  • Potential difference: Voltage across a circuit (unit: Volt, V).
  • Measured by voltmeter, connected in parallel.
  • Examples:
    • 10 J work moves 2 C: V = 10/2 = 5 V.
    • 20 J for 4 C: V = 20/4 = 5 V.
    • 15 J for 3 C: V = 15/3 = 5 V.
Electric Circuit
  • Closed path for current flow (source, conductor, load).
  • Components: Battery, wires, resistor, switch.
  • Examples:
    • Battery + bulb + switch circuit.
    • Resistor + ammeter + voltmeter circuit.
    • LED + battery + wires circuit.
Ohm's Law
  • V = IR (Voltage = Current × Resistance).
  • Resistance (R): Opposition to current flow, unit: Ohm (Ω).
  • Examples:
    • V = 12 V, I = 4 A: R = 12/4 = 3 Ω.
    • V = 6 V, R = 2 Ω: I = 6/2 = 3 A.
    • I = 2 A, R = 5 Ω: V = 2 × 5 = 10 V.
Resistance and Resistivity
  • Resistance: R = ρL/A (ρ = resistivity, L = length, A = area).
  • Resistivity: Property of material, unit: Ω·m.
  • Factors: Material, length, area, temperature.
  • Examples:
    • Copper wire: ρ = 1.68 × 10⁻⁸ Ω·m, low resistance.
    • Nichrome wire: High ρ, used in heaters.
    • Longer wire (L doubles): R doubles.
Series and Parallel Circuits
  • Series: Same current, total R = R₁ + R₂ + ...
  • Parallel: Same voltage, 1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ...
  • Examples:
    • Series: R₁ = 2 Ω, R₂ = 3 Ω, Total R = 5 Ω.
    • Parallel: R₁ = 4 Ω, R₂ = 4 Ω, Total R = 2 Ω.
    • Parallel: R₁ = 6 Ω, R₂ = 3 Ω, Total R = 2 Ω.
Electric Power
  • Rate of energy consumption, P = VI = I²R = V²/R.
  • Unit: Watt (W), 1 kW = 1000 W.
  • Examples:
    • V = 220 V, I = 2 A: P = 220 × 2 = 440 W.
    • I = 5 A, R = 10 Ω: P = 5² × 10 = 250 W.
    • V = 12 V, R = 3 Ω: P = 12²/3 = 48 W.
Electric Energy
  • Energy = Power × Time (E = P × t).
  • Unit: Joule (J), commercial unit: kWh (1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J).
  • Examples:
    • P = 1000 W, t = 1 h: E = 1 kWh.
    • P = 500 W, t = 2 h: E = 1 kWh.
    • P = 200 W, t = 5 h: E = 1 kWh.
Heating Effect of Electric Current
  • Heat produced: H = I²Rt (Joule’s law).
  • Applications: Electric iron, heater, bulb.
  • Examples:
    • I = 2 A, R = 5 Ω, t = 60 s: H = 2² × 5 × 60 = 1200 J.
    • I = 3 A, R = 4 Ω, t = 30 s: H = 3² × 4 × 30 = 1080 J.
    • I = 1 A, R = 10 Ω, t = 100 s: H = 1² × 10 × 100 = 1000 J.
Domestic Electric Circuits
  • Main supply: 220–240 V, 50 Hz AC.
  • Components: Fuse, MCB, earthing, live/neutral/earth wires.
  • Examples:
    • Fuse prevents overload in home wiring.
    • MCB trips during short circuit.
    • Earthing protects from electric shock.
