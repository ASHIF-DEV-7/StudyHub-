Control and Coordination

Nervous System

Nervous System

  • Definition: Network of neurons controlling body responses via electrical impulses.
  • Components: Brain, spinal cord, nerves; neurons (sensory, motor, relay).
  • Function: Receives stimuli, processes information, coordinates actions.
  • Examples:
    • Touching a hot surface triggers reflex (withdrawal reflex).
    • Eyes detect light, brain interprets image (vision).
    • Smelling food, brain signals hunger (salivation).
Reflex Action

Reflex Action

  • Definition: Involuntary, rapid response to a stimulus via reflex arc.
  • Pathway: Receptor → Sensory neuron → Spinal cord → Motor neuron → Effector.
  • Significance: Protects from harm, requires no conscious thought.
  • Examples:
    • Knee-jerk reflex when tapped (patellar reflex).
    • Pulling hand from hot object (withdrawal reflex).
    • Blinking when dust enters eye (blink reflex).
Human Brain

Human Brain

  • Definition: Central control unit of nervous system, processes stimuli.
  • Parts: Cerebrum (thinking, memory), Cerebellum (balance), Medulla (involuntary actions).
  • Function: Controls voluntary actions, reflexes, emotions, memory.
  • Examples:
    • Cerebrum: Solving a math problem.
    • Cerebellum: Maintaining balance while cycling.
    • Medulla: Regulating heartbeat during exercise.
Coordination in Plants

Coordination in Plants

  • Definition: Chemical coordination via plant hormones (no nervous system).
  • Types: Tropism (directional, e.g., phototropism), Nastic movements (non-directional).
  • Hormones: Auxin (growth), Gibberellin (stem elongation), Abscisic acid (stress response).
  • Examples:
    • Sunflower bends toward light (phototropism).
    • Mimosa pudica leaves close on touch (thigmotropism).
    • Roots grow downward (geotropism).
Hormones in Animals

Hormones in Animals

  • Definition: Chemical messengers secreted by endocrine glands, regulating body functions.
  • Examples: Adrenaline (fight/flight), Thyroxine (metabolism), Insulin (blood sugar).
  • Function: Coordinate growth, metabolism, reproduction, stress response.
  • Examples:
    • Adrenaline increases heart rate during danger.
    • Insulin lowers blood sugar after a meal.
    • Thyroxine boosts metabolism in cold weather.
