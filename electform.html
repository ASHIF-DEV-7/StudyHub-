Ohm's Law
V = I × R
Where:
V = Voltage (Potential Difference)
I = Current (Flow of Charge)
R = Resistance (Opposition to Current)
V: Volt, I: Ampere, R: Ohm
Electric Current
I = Q/t
Where:
I = Electric Current
Q = Electric Charge
t = Time Period
I: Ampere, Q: Coulomb, t: Second
Resistance of a Conductor
R = ρL/A
Where:
R = Resistance
ρ = Resistivity (Material Property)
L = Length of Conductor
A = Cross-sectional Area
R: Ohm, ρ: Ohm-meter, L: meter, A: m²
Series Combination
Rtotal = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...
In Series:
• Total resistance = Sum of all resistances
• Current remains same
• Voltage gets divided
All resistances in Ohms
Parallel Combination
1/Rtotal = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + ...
In Parallel:
• Reciprocal of total = Sum of reciprocals
• Voltage remains same
• Current gets divided
All resistances in Ohms
Electrical Work & Energy
W = V × I × t = V × Q
Where:
W = Work Done / Energy Consumed
V = Voltage Applied
I = Current Flowing
t = Time Duration
Q = Total Charge
W: Joule, V: Volt, I: Ampere, t: Second

Power using Voltage & Resistance

💡Key Points to Remember

• 1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J (Unit of electrical energy)

• Fuse Rating = Maximum safe current

• Short Circuit occurs when R → 0, causing I → ∞

• Heating Effect: H = I²Rt (Joule's Law)

Joule's Law of Heating
H = I² × R × t
Where:
H = Heat Produced
I = Current through conductor
R = Resistance of conductor
t = Time duration
H: Joule, I: Ampere, R: Ohm, t: Second
Alternative Heating Formulas
H = V²t/R = VIt
Different forms:
H = V²t/R (using V and R)
H = VIt (using V, I and t)
All give the same result
H: Joule, V: Volt, I: Ampere, R: Ohm
Commercial Unit of Energy
1 kWh = 1000 W × 1 h
Conversion:
1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J
1 Unit = 1 kWh
Used in electricity bills
kWh: Kilowatt Hour
Electricity Bill Calculation
Electricity Bill Calculation
Cost = Power × Time × Rate
Where:
Power = Appliance power in kW
Time = Usage time in hours
Rate = Cost per kWh (unit)
Cost: Currency per kWh
