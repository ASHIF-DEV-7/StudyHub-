Physics

Light, Electricity & Magnetic Effects

  • Question 1
    What is the SI unit of electric current?
    Ampere (A). It is named after André-Marie Ampère and measures the flow of electric charge per unit time.
  • Question 2
    What causes the twinkling of stars?
    Atmospheric refraction. Light from stars bends as it passes through different layers of Earth's atmosphere with varying densities.
  • Question 3
    Which lens is used to correct myopia?
    Concave lens. It diverges light rays to move the image back to the retina for clear vision.
  • Question 4
    What is the formula for electrical power?
    P = V × I = I²R = V²/R. Where P is power, V is voltage, I is current, and R is resistance.
  • Question 5
    Why does a compass needle point north?
    Earth's magnetic field. The compass needle aligns with Earth's magnetic field lines, pointing toward the magnetic north pole.
  • Question 6
    What is electromagnetic induction?
    When a conductor moves in a magnetic field or when magnetic field changes around a conductor, EMF is induced. This is Faraday's Law.
  • Question 7
    Which color has the maximum wavelength in visible spectrum?
    Red light. It has the longest wavelength (about 700 nm) and lowest frequency in the visible spectrum.
  • Question 8
    What is the speed of light in vacuum?
    3 × 10⁸ m/s. This is a fundamental constant denoted by 'c' and is the maximum speed at which information can travel.
  • Question 9
    Why do we use fuses in electrical circuits?
    Safety protection. Fuses melt when excess current flows, breaking the circuit to prevent fire and damage to appliances.
  • Question 10
    What is total internal reflection?
    When light travels from denser to rarer medium and angle of incidence exceeds critical angle, complete reflection occurs at the boundary.
  • Question 11
    Which device measures electric current?
    Ammeter. It is connected in series with the circuit and has very low resistance to avoid affecting the current measurement.
  • Question 12
    What is the focal length of a plane mirror?
    Infinite. A plane mirror can be considered as a spherical mirror with infinite radius of curvature, hence infinite focal length.
  • Question 13
    Why does sky appear blue?
    Scattering of light. Blue light has shorter wavelength and gets scattered more by tiny particles in the atmosphere.
  • Question 14
    State Ohm's law.
    V = I × R. The potential difference across a conductor is directly proportional to current, provided temperature remains constant.
  • Question 15
    Which part of eye controls light entry?
    Iris. It contains muscles that contract or relax to change the size of the pupil, controlling light entry.
  • Question 16
    What is the function of a transformer?
    To change AC voltage. Step-up transformer increases voltage while step-down transformer decreases voltage using electromagnetic induction.
  • Question 17
    Why are concave mirrors used in torches?
    To produce parallel beam. When light source is placed at the focus, concave mirror reflects light as parallel rays, creating a strong beam.
  • Question 18
    What is the commercial unit of electrical energy?
    Kilowatt-hour (kWh). Also called 'unit' of electricity, equal to energy consumed by 1 kW appliance in 1 hour.
  • Question 19
    How is rainbow formed?
    Dispersion and total internal reflection. Sunlight splits into seven colors in water droplets through refraction, dispersion and reflection.
  • Question 20
    Which rule gives direction of induced current?
    Fleming's Right Hand Rule. Thumb (motion), first finger (field), middle finger (induced current) are perpendicular to each other.

Chemistry

Acids, Bases & Salts, Metals & Non-metals

  • Question 1
    What is the pH of pure water?
    pH = 7. Pure water is neutral with equal concentration of H⁺ and OH⁻ ions.
  • Question 2
    Which gas is produced when metals react with acids?
    Hydrogen gas (H₂). Metal + Acid → Salt + Hydrogen gas. This is a displacement reaction.
  • Question 3
    What is the chemical formula of baking soda?
    NaHCO₃ (Sodium bicarbonate). Used in cooking, fire extinguishers, and as antacid due to its basic nature.
  • Question 4
    Which acid is present in vinegar?
    Acetic acid (CH₃COOH). About 5-8% acetic acid in water gives vinegar its sour taste and preservative properties.
  • Question 5
    What is galvanization?
    Process of coating iron with zinc. Zinc coating prevents rusting as zinc is more reactive than iron and acts as sacrificial protection.
  • Question 6
    Which element is used in pencil lead?
    Carbon (Graphite). Graphite is a soft form of carbon that leaves marks on paper due to its layered structure.
  • Question 7
    What turns lime water milky?
    Carbon dioxide (CO₂). Forms calcium carbonate precipitate: Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O
  • Question 8
    Which metal is liquid at room temperature?
    Mercury (Hg). Has melting point of -39°C and boiling point of 357°C, making it liquid at room temperature.
  • Question 9
    What is the atomic number of carbon?
    6. Carbon has 6 protons in its nucleus, giving it atomic number 6 and electronic configuration 2,4.
  • Question 10
    Which process separates salt from seawater?
    Distillation. Water evaporates leaving salt behind, then water vapor is condensed to get pure water.
  • Question 11
    What is the chemical name of common salt?
    Sodium chloride (NaCl). Formed by ionic bonding between sodium (Na⁺) and chloride (Cl⁻) ions.
  • Question 12
    Which gas is used in fire extinguishers?
    Carbon dioxide (CO₂). Being heavier than air, it covers the fire and cuts off oxygen supply, stopping combustion.
  • Question 13
    What is the hardest naturally occurring substance?
    Diamond. Carbon atoms in tetrahedral arrangement create extremely strong covalent bonds, hardness = 10 on Mohs scale.
  • Question 14
    Which indicator turns pink in basic solution?
    Phenolphthalein. Colorless in acidic/neutral solutions, turns pink/magenta in basic solutions (pH > 8.2).
  • Question 15
    What is the valency of carbon?
    4. Carbon has 4 electrons in outer shell, can form 4 covalent bonds to achieve stable octet configuration.
  • Question 16
    Which compound is known as 'laughing gas'?
    Nitrous oxide (N₂O). Used as anesthetic in dentistry, causes euphoria and laughter when inhaled in small amounts.
  • Question 17
    What is the main ore of aluminum?
    Bauxite (Al₂O₃·2H₂O). Aluminum is extracted from bauxite through electrolytic reduction after purification.
  • Question 18
    Which type of reaction releases heat?
    Exothermic reaction. Energy is released as heat, light, or sound. Examples: combustion, respiration, neutralization.
  • Question 19
    What is sublimation?
    Direct conversion of solid to gas. No liquid phase involved. Examples: dry ice, camphor, iodine crystals.
  • Question 20
    Which element has chemical symbol 'Au'?
    Gold. Symbol derived from Latin 'Aurum'. Gold is unreactive, doesn't tarnish, and has atomic number 79.

Biology

Life Processes, Control & Coordination

  • Question 1
    What is the basic unit of life?
    Cell. The smallest structural and functional unit of all living organisms, discovered by Robert Hooke in 1665.
  • Question 2
    Which organelle is called the powerhouse of the cell?
    Mitochondria. Produces ATP (energy) through cellular respiration. Has double membrane and its own DNA.
  • Question 3
    What is photosynthesis?
    Process where plants make food using sunlight. 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (in chloroplasts)
  • Question 4
    Which blood group is universal donor?
    O negative (O-). Has no A, B, or Rh antigens, so can donate to all blood groups without causing immune reaction.
  • Question 5
    What is the function of stomata?
    Gas exchange and transpiration. Tiny pores on leaves controlled by guard cells for CO₂ entry and O₂/water vapor exit.
  • Question 6
    Which hormone regulates blood sugar?
    Insulin. Produced by pancreas, helps cells absorb glucose from blood, deficiency causes diabetes mellitus.
  • Question 7
    What is the longest bone in human body?
    Femur (thigh bone). Extends from hip to knee, strongest bone supporting body weight during walking and running.
  • Question 8
    Which part of brain controls balance?
    Cerebellum. Located behind the brain stem, coordinates voluntary movements, balance, posture, and motor learning.
  • Question 9
    What are chromosomes?
    Thread-like structures carrying genes. Made of DNA and proteins, humans have 23 pairs (46 total) in each cell nucleus.
  • Question 10
    Which organ purifies blood?
    Kidneys. Filter waste products and excess water from blood to form urine, maintain electrolyte balance and blood pressure.
  • Question 11
    What is transpiration?
    Loss of water vapor from plants. Mainly through stomata, helps in water absorption, nutrient transport, and cooling.
  • Question 12
    Which vitamin is produced by skin?
    Vitamin D. Synthesized when skin is exposed to UVB radiation from sunlight, essential for calcium absorption and bone health.
  • Question 13
    What is the functional unit of kidney?
    Nephron. Microscopic filtering unit consisting of glomerulus and tubules, each kidney has about 1 million nephrons.
  • Question 14
    Which gas is released during photosynthesis?
    Oxygen (O₂). Released as byproduct when water molecules split during light reactions, essential for all aerobic life.
  • Question 15
    What controls the pupil size?
    Iris. Colored part of eye with muscles that contract/relax to change pupil diameter, controlling light entry into eye.
  • Question 16
    Which blood vessels carry blood away from heart?
    Arteries. Have thick muscular walls to withstand high pressure, carry oxygenated blood (except pulmonary artery).
  • Question 17
    What is the site of protein synthesis?
    Ribosomes. Small organelles that translate mRNA into proteins, found free in cytoplasm or attached to rough ER.
  • Question 18
    Which part of plant absorbs water?
    Roots. Root hairs increase surface area for water and mineral absorption from soil through osmosis and active transport.
  • Question 19
    What is the master gland?
    Pituitary gland. Controls other endocrine glands by secreting hormones, located at base of brain, size of a pea.
  • Question 20
    Which tissue transports food in plants?
    Phloem. Vascular tissue that transports sugars and organic compounds from leaves to other parts of plant (translocation).
